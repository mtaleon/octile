## B) 2–3 分鐘長篇（Long Story）

**繁中**：  
故事從 250 BC 開始。阿基米德把正方形切成 14 塊象牙，提出 Stomachion：不是問「拼不拼得回去」，而是問——**到底有多少種「不同」的拼法？** 這個問題不只是益智遊戲，它觸及的是「組合」與「對稱」：當兩個解只是旋轉或鏡像，它們究竟算不算同一個解？  
這份追問橫跨了兩千多年，直到 2003 年 Bill Cutler 借助電腦，才確定 Stomachion 的答案：**536** 種獨特解。那一刻，人類終於用運算把古典幾何的直覺，推進到可驗證的枚舉。 [\[github.com\]](https://github.com/mtaleon/octile)

Octile 就是在這條脈絡上誕生的。它把「填滿正方形」翻譯成更現代、也更可玩的一種語言：一個 8×8 的棋盤、11 塊板塊（從 1×1 到 3×4），要填滿 64 格。三塊先放好，剩下八塊交給你——不重疊、不留白。  
但 Octile 的野心不只在玩法，而在題庫：我們同樣問了阿基米德的問題——**有多少題是真正「不同」的題？** 於是 Octile 透過完整枚舉搜尋所有可能的題目，並以 **D4 對稱**（旋轉與鏡像）去除重複，最終得到 **11,378** 題。每一題都可解、每一題都唯一——不是隨機生成，而是被「發現」。 [\[github.com\]](https://github.com/mtaleon/octile)

你每完成一題，做的其實不是消磨時間，而是在參與一段跨越兩千年的數學傳統：  
**把複雜拆成可解的部分，把直覺變成可驗證的答案。** [\[github.com\]](https://github.com/mtaleon/octile)

**EN**:  
The story begins in 250 BC. Archimedes cut a square into 14 ivory pieces and posed Stomachion—not “Can you reassemble the square?” but **How many truly different ways can it be done?** The question isn’t mere entertainment; it’s about combinatorics and symmetry: if two solutions differ only by rotation or reflection, are they really different?  
That challenge persisted for millennia. Only in 2003 did Bill Cutler, aided by computers, determine the answer: **536** unique solutions—turning classical geometric intuition into verifiable enumeration. [\[github.com\]](https://github.com/mtaleon/octile)

Octile is born from that same lineage. It translates “fill the square” into a modern, playable language: an 8×8 board and 11 tiles (from 1×1 to 3×4) that must cover all 64 cells. Three are placed up front; the remaining eight are yours—no overlaps, no gaps.  
But Octile’s ambition goes beyond gameplay—it’s about the puzzle universe itself. We asked Archimedes’ question again: **How many puzzles are truly distinct?** So Octile performs an exhaustive search across the space of puzzles and removes duplicates under **D4 symmetry** (rotations and reflections). The result is **11,378** puzzles—each solvable, each unique. Not randomly generated—discovered. [\[github.com\]](https://github.com/mtaleon/octile)

Every time you solve one, you’re not just passing time—you’re participating in a 2,000-year mathematical tradition:  
**breaking complexity into solvable parts, and turning intuition into proof.** [\[github.com\]](https://github.com/mtaleon/octile)
